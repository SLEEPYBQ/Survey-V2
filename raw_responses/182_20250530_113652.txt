## involved_stakeholder  
Answer: Health professionals and workers (e.g., registered nurses, nursing aides, occupational therapists, social workers, psychologists, physicians, psychiatrists, administrators, and managers) in long-term care facilities  
Source: "health professionals, care workers and management personnel including registered nurses, nursing aides, occupational therapists, physiological therapists, social workers, psychologists, physicians, psychiatrists, administrators, and managers"  

## sample_size  
Answer: 416 health professionals  
Source: "A total of 416 health professionals responded to the online survey."  

## country  
Answer: Taiwan  
Source: "investigate the attitudes of Taiwanese health personnel in long-term care towards the use of social robots for older adults."  

## age  
Answer: Mean age of 39.16 years (SD=11.37)  
Source: "The mean age of respondents was 39.16 years (SD=11.37)."  

## gender  
Answer: 85.8% female, 14.2% male  
Source: "About 85.8% of the respondents were female, and 14.2% were male."  

## demographic_background  
Answer: Majority had college or above education (75.9%); specialties included registered nurses (43.5%), nursing aides (31.7%), social workers (8.7%), and others; worked in nursing homes (56.5%), residential aged care (25.7%), home care (12.7%), or rehabilitation wards (5.0%)  
Source: "The majority of respondents (75.9%) had obtained college or above education. Registered nurses (43.5%) and nursing aides (31.7%) were the majority of respondents. Over half of the respondents (56.5%) were working in nursing homes, and 25.7% were working in residential aged care."

## needs_and_expectations  
Answer: Health personnel viewed social robots as beneficial and practical in psychosocial care for older adults, providing companionship, support, and improving treatment efficiency.  
Source: "Most health personnel had positive ATTUSRs in long-term care facilities as they viewed social robots as beneficial and practical in psychosocial care for older adults."

Source: "A cross-sectional design was used to conduct this multi-phase study."

## design_goal  
Answer: To measure health personnel attitudes towards social robots and facilitate their integration into care  
Source: "This study strives to support nursing work by providing insights into health personnel's perceptions of social robots, in order to integrate social robots into the care and lives of older adults."  

## robot_concern_function  
Answer: Functions included companionship, support for daily activities, diagnosis assistance, and improving treatment efficiency  
Source: "social robots could help with treatment of older people with mental illness," "social robots could represent companions for older adults," "social robots could help with the process of diagnosis of people with mental illness."  

## facilitating_functions  
Answer: Companionship, support, diagnosis assistance, and treatment efficiency were viewed positively as they enhance care quality and reduce workload.  
Source: "social robots could provide support, companionship, and benefits for older adults in LTC."  

## inhibitory_functions  
Answer: Some concerns about robots being a threat to aged care services (22.1% agreed) and limited belief in cost reduction (47.4% agreed)  
Source: "only 47.4% of health personnel considered that using them could reduce health/nursing treatment costs," "22.1% agreed that social robots could be a threat for aged care services."  

## stakeholder_facilitating_characteristics  
Answer: Awareness of social robots and working in nursing homes were associated with more positive attitudes.  
Source: "Attitudes of respondents towards the use of social robots for older adults in LTC were found to be positively and significantly correlated with their awareness of the use of social robots in nursing homes."  

## stakeholder_inhibitory_characteristics  
Answer: Working in residential aged care was associated with less positive attitudes.  
Source: "respondents working in nursing homes had significantly higher positive attitude scores towards social robots than those in residential aged care."

Answer: N/A  
Source: Not found in the paper  

## acceptance  
Answer: Most health personnel had positive attitudes, with 84.4% agreeing robots make care work more interesting.  
Source: "84.4% of respondents agreed or strongly agreed that using social robots in health/nursing care practice could make the care work more interesting."  

## trust  
Answer: 63% believed robots would not pose threats to mental health services.  
Source: "Most health personnel considered that robots would not pose threats to mental health services designed for older adults (63%; item 15)."  

## key_findings  
Answer: The ATTUSR-C questionnaire is valid and reliable; health personnel generally had positive attitudes towards social robots, especially in nursing homes and among those aware of robots.  
Source: "The ATTUSR-C questionnaire had good validity and reliability," "Most health personnel had positive ATTUSRs in long-term care facilities."  

## additional_info  
Answer: Limitations include gender bias (85.8% female respondents), lack of behavioral intent measurement, and limited real-world robot exposure.  
Source: "the majority of online survey respondents were female (85.8%)," "they had limited exposure to and experience in the use of social robots."  

## testing_context  
Answer: Online survey conducted in Taiwan  
Source: "Data were collected in Taiwan without any location limitation via an anonymous online survey."
