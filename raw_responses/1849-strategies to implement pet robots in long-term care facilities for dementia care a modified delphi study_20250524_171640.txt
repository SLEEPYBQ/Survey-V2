## design_goal
Answer: To support the psychosocial health of PLWD, such as reducing agitation, and improving mood and social interactions
Source: Numerous studies have demonstrated the use of pet robots as a promising psychosocial intervention for PLWD in LTC, such as reducing agitation, and improving mood and social interactions.

## robot_concern_function
Answer: Psychosocial benefits like reducing agitation and improving mood and social interactions
Source: Numerous studies have demonstrated the use of pet robots as a promising psychosocial intervention for PLWD in LTC, such as reducing agitation, and improving mood and social interactions.

## facilitating_functions
Answer: Active engagement through dialogue, practical training, and interactive assistance
Source: Experts expressed that strategies involving active engagement through dialogue, and in practical, problem-based and solution-driven training were considered pragmatic and flexible.

## inhibitory_functions
Answer: Overly bureaucratic strategies, resource straining, and lack of buy-in from local stakeholders
Source: Others were concerned about them being overly bureaucratic, complicated, or resource straining.

## stakeholder_facilitating_characteristics
Answer: Buy-in from local stakeholders, understanding organizational context, and active engagement
Source: Experts expressed that stakeholders should understand the reasons behind using pet robots and have opportunities to discuss their thoughts.

## stakeholder_inhibitory_characteristics
Answer: Resistance to change, lack of resources, and high staff turnover
Source: Experts expressed concerns about their inaccessibility and understanding of LTC context, which can lead to change resistance by local stakeholders.

## engagement
Answer: High engagement through active strategies like dialogue and practical training
Source: Experts underlined the importance of interactive, practical training.

## acceptance
Answer: Varied levels of acceptance influenced by organizational context and stakeholder buy-in
Source: Reasons for variations included contextual differences, such as resource availability, organizational structures, and staff turnover.

## trust
Answer: Gradual trust development through active engagement and practical training
Source: Experts expressed that seeing their benefits would facilitate buy-in.

## key_findings
Answer: 12 strategies were established as most relevant for implementing pet robots, focusing on local stakeholder involvement and organizational context
Source: Twelve strategies were established as being most relevant for implementing pet robots, and variations in the extent of agreement were outlined.

## additional_info
Answer: Study limitations include higher representation of UK care providers and exclusion of PLWD from the expert panel
Source: There is a higher representation of care providers from LTC organizations in the United Kingdom; however, considering that more than half of the experts were from outside the United Kingdom, our findings should be considered relevant in other national contexts.
